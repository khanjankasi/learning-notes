# Module 1 Learnings

## What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, programs, and data from cyberattacks.

### Goals of Cybersecurity

Protect:

- Devices
- Networks
- Applications
- Personal Information
- Organizational Data

---

## THE McCumber CUBE :

## CIA Triad

### Confidentiality

Ensures information is accessible only to authorized users.

Examples:

- Passwords
- Encryption
- Access Control

### Integrity

Ensures information remains accurate and unchanged.

Examples:

- Database records
- Exam marks
- Financial transactions

### Availability

Ensures systems and information remain accessible when needed.

Examples:

- Websites
- Banking systems
- Hospital databases

# Module 2 Learnings

## Vulnerability

A weakness or misconfiguration in a system.

Example:

- Weak password
- Unpatched software

## Exploit

A method used by an attacker to take advantage of a vulnerability.

Example:

- Malware exploiting a software bug

---

## Social Engineering

Manipulating people into revealing confidential information.

Examples:

- Fake support calls
- Fraudulent emails

---

## Phishing

Fraudulent messages designed to steal credentials or information.

Example:

- Fake banking emails

---

## DoS Attack

Denial of Service attack.

A single system overwhelms a target.

Result:

- Service becomes unavailable

---

## DDoS Attack

Distributed Denial of Service attack.

Multiple systems attack the target simultaneously.

Result:

- Website or service crashes

---

## Network Sniffing

Monitoring network traffic to capture transmitted data.

Risk:

- Sensitive information may be intercepted.

---

# Module 3 Learnings

## SSID

Service Set Identifier.

The name of a Wi-Fi network.

Example:

HomeWiFi

---

## WPA2

Wireless security protocol that encrypts wireless communications.

Benefits:

- Protects wireless traffic
- Reduces unauthorized access

---

## Wireless Security

Security best practices:

- Change default SSID
- Change default passwords
- Enable WPA2 encryption
- Keep firmware updated

---

# Key Takeaways

- Vulnerability = Weakness
- Exploit = Method to abuse weakness
- Social Engineering targets people
- DDoS targets availability
- WPA2 improves wireless security
- Cybersecurity requires multiple layers of protection
